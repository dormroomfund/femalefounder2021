Female Founder Track

Founderhood has a reputation for being a boys' club. Not at Dorm Room Fund.
Dorm Room Fund is excited to bring Female Founder Track to San Francisco this summer. Over six evening sessions, we’ll help you scale your project and equip you with the skills to raise your first capital. Together, we’ll craft a plan for domination, conviction, and confidence.

You'll learn about scaling MVPs, fundraising, and hiring from some of the successful founders, investors, and operators and get one-on-one feedback from mentors. Best of all, you'll build a peer group of other women in your same stage, hustling to take their startup from side project to full time gig.
Applications are now open and will close May 1st at 11:59pm PST. Click here to apply.

Increasing access.

By hosting FFT in San Francisco this summer, we hope to reach women from all types of schools and backgrounds as they intern in the city. This hands-on program will give you the skills, network of mentors, and community of other women for you to jumpstart your company.

Curriculum

Over the course of 6 weeks, the most successful female founders, investors, and operators in New York will help cohort members:

Iterate on user research and MVPs
Learn about unit economics and venture scale
Strengthen a business model and market strategies
Demystify the fundraising process
Understand positioning and personal narrative
Know when and how to build a team

FAQ

1. Who should apply?
All female and non-binary students (undergraduate or graduate, graduating May 2020 or eariler) who have at least a proof-of concept through research, MVPs, or an early-stage company. The applicant will be living in San Francisco during the summer of 2020 and can commit to the program on top of their internship, job, or summer program.

You’re probably already committing nights and weekends to your side hustle and want to use the summer to build the foundations of a venture-scale company.
2. Do I need to have a company to apply?
You don't have to be incorporated yet. However, we require a proof of concept in some form (user research, MVPs), and a commitment to develop the venture through the summer. The goal of this program is to get you ready to scale your company and ready to raise your first captial.
3. What does the application process look like?
Applications are due by Friday, May 1 at 11:59pm PST (late applications will not be accepted). Selected applicants will be given an interview during late April or early May. After the interview, decisions will be sent out before the end of May.
4. Can I be a student working on my venture full time during the summer and participate?
Yes! However, we cannot provide any sort of funding, housing, or other supporting compensation. If you are not from the Bay Area, to offset the costs, we recommend committing to this program on top of your existing internship or job for the summer.
5. Is this program an accelerator?
No. This program is designed to support the side hustlers through education and community, to explore if this venture is a viable post-grad opportunity. We don't require that students continue their venture after graduation. There is no guarantee of funding from Dorm Room Fund, First Round, or any speakers/mentors from the sessions. However, we aim to help you take a big leap in the direction of progress
6. What is Dorm Room Fund?
Built by students and powered by First Round Capital, Dorm Room Fund provide founders with a strong network of investors, world-class mentors, and a $20,000 check. We’re a venture fund that has backed over 300 startups, whose founders have gone on to raise from the top venture capital firms.
7. Where are the sessions held?
All the sessions will be held in-person at First Round's New York office.
8. When are the in-person sessions?
Sessions run from 6:30pm-8:30pm on the following Thursday evenings in July and August: 6/25, 7/9, 7/16, 7/23, 7/30, 8/6
9. What does a typical masterclass session look like?
Each session will be taught by leading expert founders, investors, and operators from the Bay Area and the First Round community. Dorm Room Fund partners will review your deliverables and provide personalized feedback.
10. What if I can't make all the sessions?
We require all participants to commit to attending all sessions. We ask about this in the application and it will be a deciding factor. We’ve prepared a lot of programming, speakers, and mentors and we wouldn’t want you to miss these amazing opportunities.
11. How much does it cost to participate in this program?
Nothing. At Dorm Room Fund, we’re working to support student founders and making the top 10% of founders more diverse. We will provide you with the knowledge to launch your company if you are accepted into the Female Founder Track cohort. We believe in making entrepreneurship as accessible as possible.
12. Who can I contact if I have more questions?
Please reach out to Havi Nguyen at havi@dormroomfund.com or Isabelle Zhou at isabelle@dormroomfund.com. Don’t hesitate to ask any questions!
